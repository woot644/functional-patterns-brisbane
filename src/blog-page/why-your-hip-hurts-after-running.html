Why Your Hip Hurts After Running

Written by Louis Ellery • 2026-04-21

Hip pain after running is rarely about the hip. The tissue that complains is downstream of a pattern further up the chain — usually in how the thorax rotates, or in where you land under your centre of mass.

When you decelerate on a stride, the hip socket should absorb that force through internal rotation. If your rib cage is stuck — fixed in inhalation, or rotated the same direction as your pelvis — the hip can't rotate underneath you. You end up compressing the joint instead of using it.

This is why 'stretching the hip' rarely helps. You're chasing the symptom, not the pattern. We assess for this directly in the 90-minute posture + gait assessment, and the correction work starts at the thorax, not the hip.
