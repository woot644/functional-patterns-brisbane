# Hunchback Posture

---

## Posture isn't a position. It's the result of how your body moves.

---

## Why Exercises Alone Don't Fix It

Conventional "posture exercises" like rows, band pull-aparts, and chin tucks treat hunchback posture as a muscular weakness problem. But if weak muscles were the cause, strengthening them would fix it — and for most people, it doesn't. The issue is deeper than muscle strength.

---

## What Is Hunchback Posture?

Hunchback posture — clinically known as **thoracic kyphosis** — involves:

- **Excessive thoracic curvature** — rounding of the upper back
- **Forward head posture** — the head shifts forward of the centre of gravity
- **Ribcage compression** — the rib cage collapses, restricting breathing and organ function

---

## Root Causes

Hunchback posture develops from accumulated movement dysfunction — years of compensatory patterns that pull the spine into excessive flexion. These patterns are embedded in how you walk, breathe, and stabilise your body, not just how you sit at a desk.

---

## 3-Component Treatment Approach

1. **Myofascial release** — releasing the fascial restrictions that hold the spine in kyphosis
2. **Gait retraining** — rebuilding walking mechanics to create extension and rotation through the thoracic spine
3. **Corrective training** — progressive exercises that integrate the entire kinetic chain to support an upright posture
