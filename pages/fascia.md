---

## What Is Fascia?

Fascia is the connective tissue that runs throughout your entire body — wrapping muscles, organs, nerves, and bones in a continuous web. It is not just passive wrapping; fascia is an active, living tissue that transmits force, stores energy, and plays a critical role in how your body moves and feels.

---

## How Fascia Influences Chronic Conditions

When fascia becomes restricted, dehydrated, or adhered, it can:

- Create chronic pain that doesn't respond to conventional treatment
- Drive chronic inflammation by restricting blood flow and lymphatic drainage
- Contribute to chronic fatigue by forcing muscles to work harder against fascial restriction
- Refer pain to areas far from the actual restriction site

## Movement Philosophy

### Natural Human Movements vs Barbell Exercises

The Functional Patterns approach prioritises movements that align with how humans are biologically designed to move — walking, running, throwing, and the transitions between them. These natural movement patterns load fascia in the way it was designed to be loaded.

Traditional barbell exercises often load the body in linear, isolated patterns that do not reflect how fascia functions. This can reinforce existing dysfunctions rather than resolving them.
