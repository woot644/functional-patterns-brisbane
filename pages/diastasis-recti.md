## Diastasis Recti & Coning Aren't About Weak Abs — They're About Pressure & Movement Patterns

Diastasis recti is not simply a muscle separation problem. It is a **pressure and movement sequencing problem**. The abdominal wall separates because of how pressure is being managed (or mismanaged) through the trunk — not because the muscles are "weak."

---

## 6-Part Approach

### 1. Rib Cage Position
Restoring the rib cage to its optimal position so that pressure distributes evenly through the trunk.

### 2. Pelvic Orientation
Correcting pelvic tilt and rotation to support proper abdominal wall function.

### 3. Diaphragm-TVA Coordination
Re-establishing the coordination between the diaphragm and transversus abdominis for effective pressure management.

### 4. Oblique/Sling Integration
Integrating the oblique slings that wrap around the trunk to provide dynamic stability.

### 5. Gait Sequencing
Rebuilding walking mechanics to load the abdominal wall correctly during movement.

### 6. Real-World Movement
Progressing to functional, real-world movements that maintain and reinforce abdominal wall integrity.

---

## Pathways

### Pregnancy
Strategies to manage intra-abdominal pressure during pregnancy, reducing the severity of diastasis recti and supporting the body through changing demands.

### Postpartum
Structured rehabilitation to restore abdominal wall function after birth, addressing the pressure and movement patterns that prevent natural healing.
