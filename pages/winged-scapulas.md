## What Are Winged Scapulas?

Winged scapulas occur when the shoulder blades protrude from the back rather than sitting flat against the rib cage. This is not just a cosmetic issue — it indicates deeper dysfunction in how the muscles of the upper back, shoulders, and rib cage integrate and function together.

---

## Key Concepts

### Muscle Integration
Winged scapulas are a sign that the muscles meant to stabilise the shoulder blade are not integrating properly with the rest of the kinetic chain. Isolated "scapula exercises" rarely fix the problem because the dysfunction originates elsewhere.

### Rib Cage Compression
Rib cage position and mobility play a critical role. When the rib cage is compressed or rotated, the scapula cannot sit properly against it — no amount of serratus anterior work will fix a rib cage problem.
